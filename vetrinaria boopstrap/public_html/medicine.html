Veterinary medicine. CANO VETERINARY.

Veterinary medicine is the branch of medicine that deals with the prevention, diagnosis and treatment of diseases, disorders and injuries in non-human animals. The scope of veterinary medicine is broad, covering all species, both domestic and wild. The university professional who puts this science into practice is called a veterinarian, a veterinarian or a veterinary surgeon, 2 while in some Latin American countries the professional engaged in agricultural productivity is called a zootenista. The technical professional is called veterinary technician or veterinary nurse.

History. MEDICINE

The Egyptian papyrus of Lahun (1900 BC) and the literature Vedas of ancient India offer one of the earliest written records on veterinary medicine. The Buddhist emperor of India, Aśoka, ordered the following: "In all parts of the kingdom there will be two types of medicines (चिकित्सा), medicine for people and medicine for animals.Where there were no healing herbs for people and animals, It is ordered to buy and sow them. " Early attempts to organize and regulate veterinary practice tend to focus on horses because of their importance as a means of transport and a weapon of war. During the Middle Ages (1356), London Mayor Henry Picard, concerned about the poor quality of care given to horses in the city, asked that all smiths operating within a radius of seven miles from the city Form a "scholarship" to regulate and improve their practices. This last instance led to the creation of the Guild of blacksmiths in 1674.7 The first complete treatise on the anatomy of a non-human species corresponds to the book Anatomy of the Cavallo (Anatomy of the Horse), published by the Italian Carlo Ruini in 1598.8.
