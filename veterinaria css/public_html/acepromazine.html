Acepromazine

Acepromazine is a tranquilizer that depresses the central nervous system. Acepromazine is chemically classified as a neuroleptic phenothiazine substance, which means it modifies chemicals in the brain to change an animal's behavior. Acepromazine is related to chlorpromazine, which is another tranquilizer, and prochloperazine, a drug used to prevent vomiting. The exact mechanism of action of Acepromazine is unknown. It is believed to block dopamine receptors in the brain, a chemical used for communication between cells.
